Drivers:

As the lifeline of our operations, drivers undertake the responsibility of safely transporting students from their pick-up points to their respective destinations, while using their personal vehicle (Note: we do not provide vehicles, drivers should operate their own and it must be 2014 and up in a good condition with registration and insurance up to date). They diligently adhere to prescribed routes, ensuring strict compliance with safety protocols outlined. Our drivers navigate not just roads but also act as trusted guardians, aiding and ensuring students' safety throughout the journey. Our Drivers are integral to our operations, operating in alignment with school schedules. They typically handle two trips per day, usually in the morning and afternoon, with hours and workdays contingent upon the assigned route. Drivers are independent contractors and are required to file 1099 forms at the end of the fiscal year.

Payment and Deductions:

Payments are calculated based on the miles driven for each route and are executed through direct deposit on a weekly basis, every Monday with a two-week hold on every paycheck. So, the first paycheck is received on the third week of employment. The pay rate for each route varies, differing between school districts, yet typically follows a similar structure. For instance, if you drive between 1 to 16 miles, you’d receive a flat rate of $40. Beyond the 16th mile, each additional mile earns $1.30. Mileage is calculated from when you pick up the student to when you drop them off. Payment is made for each shift worked, with morning and afternoon shifts compensated separately.

To accurately calculate your earnings, it is essential to use the EverDriven driver app. The mileage calculation begins when you pick up the student from their house and ends when you drop them off at school. It is crucial to interact with the app correctly by clicking "Accept" for the route (which is available an hour before the route starts) on time, "Arrive" on time, and "Pick up" and "Drop off" on time for both morning and afternoon trips. Please note that the availability of routes at times might take longer than expected. Routes are assigned on a first-come, first-served basis, as they are randomly allocated to us as a service provider. As a result, there may be instances where our drivers experience a waiting period before securing a route. We understand the importance of minimizing downtime, and we will make every effort to get our drivers on the road as soon as routes become available.

DMV CAB Corporation facilitates a percentage-based deduction from the weekly earnings to cover services such as payroll, dispatch, and insurance. Deductions are 10% for earnings up to $650 per week and 15% for earnings surpassing $650.

Our insurance policy provides coverage for incidents/accidents occurring during work shifts while utilizing our application and is driving the same car provided to us. Additionally, our dispatch service aids in finding coverage should an employee need to take an appropriate absence from their shift. It's important to note that the pay per mile is calculated and offered to you when we present a route. As every school district may have different rates, you have the option to accept or decline the routes based on the offered pay per mile.
